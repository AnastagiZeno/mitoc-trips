What qualifications are needed to lead a Winter School trip?

Each Winter School trip must have a minimum of two leaders, at least one of whom is rated as a 'primary' leader. The Winter Safety Committe gives ratings to all trip leaders. These ratings control what kinds of trips leaders are permitted to create.

Qualifications for a particular category are a combination of outdoor experience and leadership, particularly in winter conditions.

First aid certifications

WFA or higher is required for all B and C primary leaders.

How do ratings work?

All leaders are assigned a location rating along with any applicable activity ratings.

Ratings by Location
  • A: less than one hour from intensive care (ski resorts, short hikes from the road, et cetera)
  • B: anything below treeline more than one hour from intensive care
  • C: above treeline
Ratings by Activity
  • I: ice climbing
  • S: telemark/backcountry skiing

Activity ratings are subordinate to location ratings: a {B, S} leader can only lead ski mountaineering trips below treeline. A leader rated {A, co-B, co-I} can act as primary leader on A-level trips, can co-lead B-level trips with a full B or C leader, and can co-lead ice climbing trips as long as they qualify as B level or below.

Co-leaders

Co-leaders require a primary leader to be present in the group in order to lead a trip. Co-ratings are given to those who show promise in an area but have not demonstrated the specific outdoor experience/leadership/group management necessary for a full rating. This allows them to gain experience under the supervision of an experienced leader. Co-leaders are encouraged to use the earlier weeks of Winter School to demonstrate the skill set necessary to be 'upgraded' to primary leader prior to the close of IAP.

What benefits come with being a leader?

As a trip leader, you receive free gear rentals and a course subsidy for Wilderness First Aid certification (WFA, WAFA, WFR, WEMT).

How do I become a leader?

If you're interested in leading trips, fill out a leader application form.
